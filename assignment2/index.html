DIY - Windmill Art Time!

Fun and breezy day outside, and want a simple craft to enjoy the day better with? Today we’re going to show you step-by-step how to create your very own quick and easy windmill during art time, safe and suitable for all ages!

Instructions

Part A: Making the turning part of windmill

Step 1: Cut your foam sheet into a 12.5 x 12.5cm square. Keep the scraps of foam, we will use this later.

Step 2: Fold the square diagonally in half to make a triangle Press that down, and then turn it around and fold in half diagonally the other way as well, as shown.

Step 3: Make a hole in the middle, where the creases meet. Make some cuts about halfway along each crease. Add a hole near the top of every edge of the square, as shown.

You should end up with a hole in the middle and a hole on every other side, as shown here.

Step 4: Add a bit of glue over the hole on each corner. Take each glued corner and fold them inwards to the center, to create the image shown.

Part B: Making Foam Washers

Step 5: Collecting the off-cuts of foam left over from cutting the 12.5cm x 12.5cm foam square, use a glue stick cap to press down on the foam, making a circular impression.

Step 6: Cut the circular impressions out, and put a hole straight through the middle of each.

Part C: Assembling the Windmill

Step 7: Take a pipe cleaner and wrap it around the straw so it's fairly tight. Put a washer all the way to the end of the pipe cleaner, put the turning part of the windmill, and then put another washer on top, like a sandwich.
